About DART

DART – Deceptive Assessment and Review Tracking is an AI-driven system designed to detect fake, deceptive, and AI-generated product reviews across any e-commerce platform in real time.

Home › About

The Problem

The widespread adoption of e-commerce platforms has elevated online product reviews to a critical role in consumer decision-making. As of 2025, approximately 30% of all online reviews are estimated to be fake, with 82% of consumers reporting they have encountered deceptive reviews in the past year.

In 2024 alone, Amazon blocked over 275 million fake reviews. Globally, fake reviews are estimated to influence $152 billion in e-commerce spending annually. AI-generated reviews are growing at 80% month-over-month since June 2023, rendering many traditional detection methods obsolete.

What DART Does

DART operates through a unified, multi-layered detection framework that simultaneously evaluates three distinct dimensions of review authenticity:

  • NLP Text Analysis – Transformer-based language models detect manipulated sentiment, AI-generated content, keyword stuffing, and unnatural linguistic uniformity.
  • Behavioral Analytics – Reviewer credibility profiles are built from posting frequency, temporal clustering, rating distribution asymmetry, account age, and cross-account behavioral similarities.
  • Unified Risk Scoring – All signals are synthesized into a single 0–100 deception score — LOW, MEDIUM, or HIGH — with human-readable justifications and consumer recommendations.

General Objective

To design, develop, and evaluate DART, an AI-driven, cross-platform system that automatically detects, classifies, and facilitates the moderation of deceptive product reviews in e-commerce environments in real time. The system integrates NLP-based text analysis, behavioral analytics, and a unified risk scoring and prescriptive moderation engine.

Specific Objectives

  • Develop an NLP-based text analysis module using transformer models for deceptive and AI-generated review detection
  • Design a consumer-facing review analysis module for real-time URL-based credibility analysis
  • Create an administrator module with a centralized dashboard for review monitoring and moderation
  • Implement behavioral analytics for reviewer credibility assessment
  • Develop and evaluate a unified risk scoring and performance framework (precision, recall, F1-score, accuracy)

Research Methodology

This study employs a developmental research design using the Agile Software Development Methodology. The core AI engine is built through an iterative Machine Learning lifecycle — training on publicly available labeled authentic and deceptive review datasets, supplemented by synthetic data generation. Transformer-based NLP models are fine-tuned for review classification, while behavioral features are used to train anomaly detection models.

System evaluation uses standard classification metrics: precision, recall, F1-score, and accuracy — validated through unit testing, integration testing, and User Acceptance Testing (UAT).
