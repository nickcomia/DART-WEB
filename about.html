About DART

DART – Deceptive Assessment and Review Tracking is an AI-driven, cross-platform system engineered to automatically detect, analyze, classify, and manage deceptive product reviews in real time across e-commerce environments.

Background

The widespread adoption of e-commerce platforms as the dominant channel for retail commerce has elevated online product reviews to a critical role in the consumer decision-making process. As these platforms have scaled, the review ecosystem has become an increasingly attractive target for systematic manipulation.

Deceptive reviews now encompass fabricated positive reviews, orchestrated negative reviews targeting competitors, and large-scale AI-generated synthetic reviews. As of 2025, approximately 30% of all online reviews are estimated to be fake, with 82% of consumers reporting encounters with deceptive reviews in the past year.

In 2024 alone, Amazon blocked or removed over 275 million fake reviews. Fake reviews are estimated to directly influence $152 billion in global e-commerce spending annually.

The DART System

DART operates through a unified, multi-layered detection framework that simultaneously evaluates three distinct dimensions of review authenticity:

  • Layer 1 – NLP Text Analysis: Transformer-based language models perform deep linguistic analysis, detecting manipulated sentiment polarity, AI-generated content patterns, keyword stuffing, and statistically improbable language uniformity.
  • Layer 2 – Behavioral Analytics: Reviewer credibility profiles are built by examining posting frequency, temporal clustering, asymmetric rating distributions, and cross-account behavioral similarities indicative of coordinated manipulation.
  • Layer 3 – Unified Risk Scoring: All signals are synthesized into a single deception probability score with LOW, MEDIUM, and HIGH classification, accompanied by interpretable justifications and prescriptive moderation recommendations.

General Objective

This study aims to design, develop, and evaluate DART – Deceptive Assessment and Review Tracking, an AI-driven, cross-platform system that automatically detects, classifies, and facilitates the moderation of deceptive product reviews in e-commerce environments in real time.

Specific Objectives

  • Develop an NLP-based text analysis module using transformer models for deceptive and AI-generated review detection
  • Design a consumer-facing review analysis module for real-time URL-based credibility analysis
  • Create an administrator module with a centralized dashboard for review monitoring and moderation
  • Implement behavioral analytics for reviewer credibility assessment
  • Develop and evaluate a unified risk scoring and system performance framework

Research Methodology

This study employs a developmental research design using the Agile Software Development Methodology, organized into iterative sprints. The core analytical engine is developed through an iterative Machine Learning lifecycle using transformer-based NLP models, perceptual hashing for image verification, and anomaly detection models for behavioral analysis.

System performance is evaluated using standard classification metrics: precision, recall, F1-score, and overall accuracy — validated through functional testing, integration testing, and User Acceptance Testing (UAT) with end-users.
